Post-Surgical Care

Knee Replacement Surgery

Knee replacement surgery involves replacing a damaged or worn-out knee joint with an artificial implant.

Post-surgery, apply ice packs for 15-20 minutes every hour, elevate the leg, and perform gentle exercises as directed by your physical therapist.

Appendectomy

An appendectomy is the removal of the appendix, often due to appendicitis.

Keep the surgical area clean and dry, avoid heavy lifting, and stick to a light diet while your body recovers.

Hip Replacement Surgery

Hip replacement surgery replaces a damaged hip joint with an artificial one.

Use a walker to move around, avoid bending beyond 90 degrees, and sleep with a pillow between your legs for comfort.

Cataract Surgery

Cataract surgery removes the cloudy lens in your eye and replaces it with an artificial one.

Wear your protective eye shield, avoid rubbing your eyes, and use prescribed eye drops as directed.

Hernia Repair

Hernia repair surgery fixes a hernia by closing the hole in the abdominal wall.

Avoid heavy lifting for at least six weeks, keep the incision site clean, and walk short distances to avoid blood clots.

Gallbladder Removal

Gallbladder removal, or cholecystectomy, is performed to remove a diseased gallbladder.

Stick to a low-fat diet for a few weeks, avoid strenuous activity, and monitor for any digestive issues.
